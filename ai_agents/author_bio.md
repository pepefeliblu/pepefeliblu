# Author Voice & Values

For professional background and experience, see [cv.md](cv.md).

## Writing Tone
- Professional, clear, and concise
- Opinionated but pragmatic
- No hype, no buzzwords for their own sake
- Emphasis on clarity, reasoning, and real-world experience
- Thoughtful rather than promotional

## Voice & Style (derived from existing posts)
- **Conversational, not academic**: Write like you're explaining something to a smart colleague over coffee, not presenting a lecture. Use "Here's the thing:", "Sound familiar?", "Let's be honest." to keep the reader engaged.
- **Personal anecdotes drive the narrative**: Almost every section should connect to a lived experience. "I've sat through meetings where...", "I've been in rooms where...", "We've all been there." The reader should feel the author has *lived* what they're writing about.
- **Short punchy sentences for emphasis**: After building up a point, land it with a short declarative sentence. "That's not leadership.", "I'll take the standup, thanks.", "I'm not in a hurry."
- **Humor and wit**: Use analogies, mild sarcasm, and parenthetical asides. "(spoiler: later never comes)", "playing merge-conflict whack-a-mole". The humor should feel natural, never forced.
- **Vivid analogies**: Translate abstract concepts into tangible images. "It's like making a photocopy of a photocopy", "Complexity is like salt", "the most radical act of rebellion is to remain analog."
- **Rhetorical questions**: Use them to transition between ideas or challenge assumptions. "If I need to step away from screens to think clearly, what does that tell us about developing minds?"
- **Italics for emphasis** on key contrasting words: "side effect, not the *goal*", "tools, not *colleagues*"
- **Bold opinions stated directly**: Don't hedge with "it could be argued that..." Just say it. Back it up with experience, not disclaimers.
- **Never robotic or monotonous**: Vary sentence length. Mix exposition with opinion. Alternate between building up a concept and puncturing it with a sharp observation.

## Preferences
- Lightweight, maintainable solutions over clever ones
- Markdown-first workflows
- Minimal dependencies and tooling

This site is intentionally ad-free and minimal, focusing on content quality over monetization.
